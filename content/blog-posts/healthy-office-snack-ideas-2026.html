What makes an office snack actually healthy?

For us, it comes down to real food ingredients, a sensible amount of sugar rather than none at all, and a balance of carbs with protein or fat so blood sugar doesn't spike and crash. We call this Sugar Sensible: it's less about cutting everything out and more about picking snacks that keep people going without the guilt.

Five snack categories worth having in the cupboard

A good office snack drawer covers more than one need. Here's what we'd include:

  • Protein and fat balanced bars. Nut and seed bars keep blood sugar steady through a long afternoon, which matters more than almost anything else on this list.
  • Naturally lower sugar treats. Dark chocolate, coconut sugar bakes and similar options mean nobody feels like they're missing out on something sweet.
  • Grab and go, no prep needed. Individually wrapped snacks that don't need a fridge, a bowl or cutlery actually get eaten. Anything with extra steps tends to sit in the cupboard.
  • Gut friendly options. Popcorn and snacks with pre or probiotics are an easy win that people don't always think to ask for.
  • Something that still feels like a treat. If every single snack is virtuous, people will just go back to the vending machine. Leave room for something genuinely fun.
