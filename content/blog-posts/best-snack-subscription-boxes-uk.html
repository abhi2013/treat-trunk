What is a snack subscription box?

A snack subscription box is a curated selection of snacks delivered to your door on a regular schedule, usually monthly. Instead of picking items yourself, the company chooses a rotating mix for you, which is how you end up discovering brands and flavours you'd never find in a supermarket.

Treat Trunk: the healthy one (that's us)

Treat Trunk is a healthy snack box built around what we call Sugar Sensible snacking: real-food ingredients, lower-GI sugars, and carbs balanced with protein or fat, so snacks feel like treats without the crash. Every box is vegan-friendly and packed with snacks from small, ethical UK brands.

Where we're honest about the fit: if you want mainstream crisps and chocolate, or snacks from a different country each month, one of the boxes below suits you better.

Graze: the portion-control one

Graze is the biggest name in UK snacking and works differently: you rate your preferences and it builds smaller, portion-controlled snack selections around them. It's a good fit for desk snacking and lighter portions, less so if you want full-size products or a strong focus on small independent brands.

LifeBox Food: the wellness one

LifeBox leans hard into plant-based wellness, with superfood-forward snacks and a strong focus on clean ingredients. If your snacking sits closest to your yoga mat, it's a lovely box. It's less family-oriented than a mixed box, and the treat factor is dialled down in favour of the wellness factor.

The Nutribox: the gluten-free one

The Nutribox focuses on gluten-free and vegan healthy snacks, which makes it the natural pick for coeliac and gluten-free households where every item needs to be safe. If gluten isn't a concern for you, a broader box gives you more variety for the money.

SnackVerse: the world-tour one

SnackVerse sends snacks from a different country each month across three box sizes. It's about discovery and fun rather than health, so expect proper sweets and crisps from around the world. A good gift for a curious eater; not the one to pick if you're trying to snack better.

TokyoTreat: the Japan one

TokyoTreat ships Japanese snacks, sweets and drinks directly from Japan. The novelty factor is unbeatable if Japanese snacks are your thing, with the trade-offs you'd expect: international shipping times and no health focus.

How do you choose the right snack subscription box?

Ask yourself three questions:

  • Health or novelty? If you want to snack better, pick a health-focused box (Treat Trunk, LifeBox, Nutribox). If you want fun and discovery, pick a world box (SnackVerse, TokyoTreat).
  • Who's eating it? A solo desk-snacker suits smaller portions (Graze, a Mini box). A household does better with a full-size mixed box that covers different tastes.
  • Any dietary needs? Gluten-free households should look at Nutribox first. Vegans have real choice: Treat Trunk boxes are vegan-friendly as standard, and LifeBox is plant-based throughout.

Is a snack subscription box worth it?

If you're already buying snacks every week, a subscription usually costs about the same as a supermarket habit while doing more: you discover new brands, portioning is built in, and with a healthy box the default option in the cupboard becomes the better one. The maths works best when the box actually matches how you snack, which is exactly why the "best" box is different for different people.
